Why Use Output Instead of Span? To be clear, functionally it doesn’t give you any real advantage. You could just use a span instead. But output is semantically correct. And that’s what HTML is about. You mark up text, hypertext, according to what it actually is. You declare what a heading is, what a paragraph is, what a dialog is, what an input is. And you can also declare what an output field is. And that has practical benefits: For accessibility: A screen reader understands this is the result and it can announce it without moving focus away from the controls that influence that result The for attribute provides relevant context: The sum is calculated based on values A and B An output element isn’t focusable by default, which is correct, even though it is inside of a form where it belongs So, good for accessibility and it makes your HTML cleaner and nicer.
