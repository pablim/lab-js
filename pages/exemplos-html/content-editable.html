Why contenteditable is powerful: Build rich text editors without any library Create inline editing interfaces (click to edit) Perfect for note-taking apps, CMSs, collaborative tools Works with document.execCommand() for formatting (bold, italic, lists, links) Can be combined with spellcheck and autocorrect attributes A word of caution: Contenteditable can be tricky with complex formatting. For production apps, you might still want a library like Quill or ProseMirror. But for simple use cases? It’s genuinely elegant and works great out of the box.
